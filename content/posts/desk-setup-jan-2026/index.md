## Camera

Webcams are cheap and lack features that you may want (like [bokeh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bokeh)). But, unless you're recording/streaming tons of content those features may be less important to you. If you want something that "just works" use your laptop's built in camera. Modern modern phones can double as a camera for  desktop use too (which you already paid for). If you want to improve picture quality on a budget, go with a 1080p or 4K webcam from [Logitech](https://www.amazon.com/stores/Logitech/page/494BEB46-B847-41B3-93AB-206A1E9CE8FD?lp_context_asin=B0DBTZZQXR&lp_context_query=logitech+streamcam&store_ref=bl_ast_dp_brandLogo_sto&ref_=cm_sw_r_ud_ast_store_GW2D9RQ07YQ195ZGFF8N
Webcams) or [Elgato](https://a.co/d/eMbvNwn).

If you want to have a very clean look with a better than webcam quality, you need to be ready to spend some money to get what you want. I use an old Sony A6000 I got off of eBay. If money was no object and I were looking to purchase something new, I'd go with the [Sony Alpha ZV-E10](https://a.co/d/5S3NIGe). But, it's $800 without a lens. If that's not your cup of tea, go with a used camera body only and seriously consider the last generation camera from a well known brand and pair it with a Sigma lens (used or new). I'm currently using the Sigma 30mm

### XLR Interface

[**Elgato Wave XLR**](https://a.co/d/2HCPUQ0)

I used to use the [Focusrite Scarlett 2i2](https://a.co/d/8Hxik8x) but that was the best of what was available in 2020 and I had gone through a few of them. Last year I switched to the [Elgato Wave XLR](https://a.co/d/2HCPUQ0) and haven't looked back. Switching to this XLR interface allowed me to pull the [Cloudlifter CL-1 Mic Activator](https://a.co/d/iicOa20) out of the circuit which gave me more room on my desk.

The Elgato Wave Link software has some useful filters but, I generally don't use the software because they can consume CPU cycles that are best spent elsewhere while potentially throwing off the sync between voice and video outputs. This configuration "just works" which is exactly what I'm going for.

### Mic Arm

[**Elgato Wave Mic Arm**](https://a.co/d/102xBIn)

While I was at Red Hat I had a real knack for breaking mic arms. I eventually landed on the [Heil Sound PL-2T Overhead Broadcast Boom](https://a.co/d/eq4K9zd) which worked well for years. But, nothing lasts forever. Last year, I noticed the mic arm was starting to get a little wobbly so a search for a replacement started. I wanted something purpose built for a desk. I didn't want to have to have things falling off the desk. More importantly, I wanted to be able to move the mic arm mount point to any part of the desk. After a long search I landed on the [Elgato Wave Mic Arm](https://a.co/d/102xBIn). There's a newer version of it, [Wave Mic Arm MK.2](https://a.co/d/bo2P7q5), but I'm not upgrading until I have to.

The detachable riser made it possible for me to actually put something on that side of the desk that would've been knocked off with a regular mic arm. The motion is fluid and quiet and I don't use a shock mount. I can spin it 360 degrees and the only thing I have to worry about is the XLR cable getting wrapped around the riser. It's the best mic arm for me and I'd highly recommend it after several months of use.

### Sound System

Oh yeah! You read that right. I have an amazing set up for listening to ANYTHING. From TV, Movies, YouTube videos, or your favorite streaming service. Voice activated or via AirPlay (or Bluetooth), I can connect every device in the house to it.

Two [Sonos Era 100s](https://a.co/d/c5pZSpL) are across the office from each other. But, that wasn't enough. The flooring in my office is sound dampening thanks to some thick insulation under it. This enabled me to get a [Sonos Sub Mini](https://a.co/d/36AbcfA) to make a 2.1 stereo sound system on steroids. Sonos has a good veteran's discount too.

## Desk

My desk has not changed since [the last time I wrote about it](/desk-setup-january-2021/#desk). I still have my [Fully Jarvis 72" Standing Desk](https://store.hermanmiller.com/brands-fully) with casters. The one thing that did change was that Jarvis was acquired by HermanMiller. If I had it to do over again, I would have gotten a natural wood desktop that was thicker to make mounting gear to it easier.

Don't sleep on having a desk on wheels. It opens options up for a number of scenarios like Nerf battles, moving in and out of the sunlight during the day, and for when you decide to rearrange your office one day on a whim.

I still have redundancies like a UPS on everything here in the house including the desk. While I was at AWS, we had the power go out during a meeting and I was still online in the meeting (in the dark).
